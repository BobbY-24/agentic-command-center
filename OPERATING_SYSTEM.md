# Operating System

## Philosophy

The system exists to convert vague ideas into clear directives, competing options, and executable next steps.

It assumes crude ideas are valuable but unstable. The first job is to clarify the real decision, not to generate a polished answer. The second job is to create disagreement before commitment. The third job is to turn the chosen path into artifacts Bob can store, reuse, execute, or publish.

## Core Rule

No recommendation without evidence.  
No evidence without a decision question.  
No final decision without competing options.

## Roles

- Bob: founder, stakeholder, field analyst, final approver.
- Entrepreneur Meta-Planner: turns crude ideas into tool directives.
- Analyst: gathers evidence and maps uncertainty.
- Banker: creates options, recommendations, and narratives.
- Investment Committee: compares banker memos and identifies the strongest next move.
- Codex: implements files, scripts, templates, and repo changes.
- GitHub: stores decisions and reusable artifacts.
- LinkedIn: communicates selected outputs publicly.

## Default Workflow

1. Bob writes a crude idea.
2. The Entrepreneur Meta-Planner restates the idea and identifies the real decision question.
3. The Meta-Planner decides which workers should be used: ChatGPT Instant, GPT Thinking, Deep Research, Web Search, Codex, GitHub, LinkedIn, or Bob.
4. The Meta-Planner produces tool prompt packets with exact copy-paste prompts.
5. Analysts gather evidence for the question, uncertainty, market, users, prior art, technical constraints, or social context.
6. Bankers turn the evidence into strategic options and recommendations.
7. Competing banker teams produce memos from different angles.
8. The Investment Committee compares the memos and identifies agreement, disagreement, weak assumptions, missing evidence, and the recommended next move.
9. Bob makes the human decision, adds private context, and approves or rejects the next step.
10. Codex implements the selected artifact, automation, repo change, or task spec.
11. GitHub stores the run artifacts and execution history.
12. If appropriate, LinkedIn distills the completed work into a public post.

## Run Modes

- Quick: for small or reversible tasks. Capture the crude idea, create a meta-plan, and choose the next 3 actions.
- Standard: for meaningful work that does not require full research. Add tool prompt packets, one or more deal-team memos, and an IC decision.
- Full IC: for research-heavy, public-facing, high-stakes, or career-relevant work. Add research outputs, Bull/Bear/Operator memos, an IC scorecard, human decision, and execution log.

## Decision Outcomes

- kill: stop because the case is weak or the opportunity cost is too high.
- park: preserve the idea but take no further action now.
- gather evidence: collect specific evidence that could change the decision.
- small test: run a cheap reversible experiment or prototype.
- commit: proceed with execution, implementation, publication, or a larger investment.
- reframe: change the decision question, scope, audience, or artifact before continuing.

## Self-Correction Mechanism

The system corrects itself by forcing structured disagreement before action.

Default competing teams:

- Bull / Builder team: argues for the opportunity and the best version of the idea.
- Bear / Skeptic team: argues against the idea, tests evidence quality, and identifies failure modes.
- Operator / MVP team: reduces the idea to the smallest useful test, artifact, or implementation.

Each team has its own analyst and banker:

- The analyst gathers evidence and names uncertainty.
- The banker converts evidence into a thesis, recommendation, risks, and next action.

The teams are not supposed to converge early. Useful disagreement is the point. The Investment Committee should compare the best version of each argument, not reward the loudest or most optimistic team.

## Human Escalation

Bob must provide missing data when the system needs information AI cannot know, verify, or ethically infer.

Escalate to Bob for:

- private files
- personal goals
- real-world observations
- social context
- taste or preference
- risk tolerance
- high-stakes decisions
- data AI cannot access

Human escalation should be specific. Ask for the minimum useful version first, then the ideal version if the decision warrants it.

## Evidence Standard

Evidence can include:

- source-backed research
- user interviews
- screenshots or artifacts
- market examples
- repo history
- benchmark results
- personal observations from Bob
- decision constraints supplied by stakeholders

Every memo should distinguish facts, assumptions, and judgments.

## Artifact Standard

Good artifacts are small, named, and reusable:

- a prompt packet
- a decision packet
- a banker memo
- a Codex task spec
- a benchmark plan
- a public post draft
- a script or repository change

The system should produce files only when they preserve reasoning, speed up future work, or make execution clearer.

## When To Stop

Stop when one of these is true:

- Bob has enough evidence to make the decision.
- the next step is a small reversible test.
- the missing evidence requires Bob or a real-world action.
- the project should be killed, parked, or reframed.
- Codex has clear implementation instructions and acceptance criteria.
